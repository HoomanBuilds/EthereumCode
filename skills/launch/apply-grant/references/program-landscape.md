# Grant Program Landscape

A curated map of the major Ethereum ecosystem grant programs. Verify current status before applying — programs open, close, change priorities, and adjust budgets frequently.

## Optimism RPGF (Retroactive Public Goods Funding)

**What it is:** Retroactive funding for public goods that have already demonstrated impact on the Optimism ecosystem.

**Who it funds:**
- Tooling that makes building on OP Stack easier
- Educational content and developer resources
- Open-source infrastructure used by multiple projects
- Community programs that grow the OP ecosystem

**Who it doesn't fund:**
- Projects that only benefit one company
- Closed-source software
- Speculative or experimental work without users

**How it works:**
1. Attest to your project's impact on the Optimism ecosystem
2. Badgeholders (community reviewers) vote on eligible projects
3. Payout is proportional to votes received
4. Prior rounds: RPGF1 ($300K), RPGF2 ($1M), RPGF3 ($10M)

**Key insight:** This is retroactive. You need to have already shipped and shown impact. Apply after building, not before.

**URL:** `https://rounds.wtf/optimism` (or current rounds URL)

## Arbitrum Foundation Grants

**What it is:** Direct grants for projects building on Arbitrum One or Arbitrum Nova.

**Who it funds:**
- DeFi protocols deploying on Arbitrum
- Developer tooling for Arbitrum
- Consumer apps that drive transaction volume
- Infrastructure that benefits Arbitrum users

**Budget range:** $10K–$250K depending on scope

**Cycle:** Rolling applications

**Key insight:** Arbitrum cares about TVL growth and transaction count. Frame your project in terms of ecosystem growth metrics.

**URL:** `https://arbitrum.foundation/grants`

## Ethereum Foundation Grants

**What it is:** Grants from the EF for protocol-level work, research, and core tooling.

**Who it funds:**
- Client development (Geth, Nethermind, Erigon, Besu)
- Cryptography research
- Formal verification
- Developer tooling that benefits the entire ecosystem
- Education and community programs

**Who it doesn't fund:**
- dApps
- Token launches
- Commercial ventures
- Projects specific to one chain or L2

**Budget range:** $5K–$50K (EF grants are typically smaller than L2 grants)

**Key insight:** EF grants are for public goods, not products. If your project generates revenue, apply to an L2 program instead.

**URL:** `https://ethereum.org/en/community/grants/`

## Gitcoin Grants

**What it is:** Quadratic funding rounds for public goods projects.

**How it works:**
1. Projects submit during an open window
2. Community donates to projects they support
3. A matching pool amplifies small donations via the quadratic formula
4. Payout at round end

**Round types:**
- Main rounds (general public goods)
- Ecosystem rounds (specific chains, e.g., "Grants for Arbitrum")
- Climate round, AI round, etc. (specialized tracks)

**Key insight:** Quadratic funding rewards broad community support, not large individual donations. 100 people donating $1 each beats 1 person donating $100.

**URL:** `https://gitcoin.co/grants`

## Uniswap Foundation Grants

**What it is:** Grants for projects building on or with Uniswap.

**Who it funds:**
- Uniswap v4 hooks
- Integrations that use Uniswap as liquidity infrastructure
- Developer tooling for Uniswap
- Research and analysis of Uniswap data

**Key insight:** Uniswap cares about liquidity depth and swap volume. Frame proposals around increasing usage of the protocol.

## Aave Grants Program

**What it is:** Grants for projects integrating with or building on Aave.

**Who it funds:**
- New market deployments
- Cross-chain integrations
- Risk analysis tools
- Developer tooling for Aave v3/v4

## Base Ecosystem Fund

**What it is:** Grants and investments for projects building on Base.

**Who it funds:**
- Consumer crypto apps
- DeFi protocols on Base
- Developer tooling
- Onchain games
- Social apps

**Key insight:** Base prioritizes consumer-facing apps that drive wallet creation and activity. Frame your project around user growth.

## Chain-Specific Programs to Watch

- **Optimism Collective:** Beyond RPGF — the delegation program pays contributors for ecosystem work
- **zkSync:** Grants for ZK-native applications
- **Polygon:** Village program + various ecosystem funds
- **Scroll:** Grants for ZK rollup ecosystem
- **Linea:** Voyage campaigns with grant components

## How to discover programs

1. Check each chain's official website — grant pages are usually under "Ecosystem" or "Community"
2. Follow @RetroPGF and @Gitcoin on Farcaster/Twitter for round announcements
3. Join chain-specific Discords — grant opportunities are often announced there first
4. Check `https://app.charmverse.io` for some programs' application portals

## Timing

- Q1: Many programs open new rounds for the year
- Q2: RPGF rounds typically run
- Q3: Gitcoin seasonal rounds
- Q4: Year-end budget flush — some programs have unspent funds to allocate
