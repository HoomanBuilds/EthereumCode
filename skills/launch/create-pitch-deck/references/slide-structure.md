# Slide Structure

The canonical 10-12 slide structure. This works for fundraising decks. Adapt for other audiences using `audience-types.md`.

## Slide 1: Cover

```
PRODUCTNAME
One-line description. A non-crypto person could understand it.

[Presenter Name] · [Role] · [Contact]
[Optional: traction stat]
```

**Design rules:**
- Product name large, clear typeface
- One line description below it
- Your contact info in smaller text
- Optional: one traction stat if it's impressive

**Time:** 30 seconds. The cover sets the tone.

## Slide 2: Problem

**What goes on it:** A specific user with a specific pain, quantified.

**Structure:**
- Name the user ("Sarah, a DeFi user with $50K in stETH")
- Describe their current workflow and its friction
- Quantify the cost ("She earns 3.1% vs. 4.7% optimal — a $800/year gap")
- One supporting stat or graphic

**Avoid:** Generic statements ("DeFi is fragmented and complex").

**Time:** 2 minutes.

## Slide 3: Solution

**What goes on it:** What you built, in one sentence and one screenshot.

**Structure:**
- One sentence: "[Product] does [thing] for [user] by [mechanism]"
- One screenshot or diagram of the product
- One stat that proves it works (if available)

**Avoid:** Buzzwords ("decentralized, permissionless, capital-efficient").

**Time:** 2 minutes.

## Slide 4: Product Demo

**What goes on it:** The product in action.

**Structure:**
- 1-2 screenshots showing the main user flow
- Captions that explain what's happening
- Optional: embedded GIF or short video

**Avoid:** Multiple screenshots that tell different stories. Pick one flow and show it completely.

**Time:** 2 minutes.

## Slide 5: How It Works

**What goes on it:** Just enough mechanism for the audience to evaluate.

**For VCs:** A flow chart with 4-5 boxes showing the user journey and value flow.

**For technical audiences:** Contract architecture, key invariants, data flow.

**For partners:** Integration diagram showing how your system connects to theirs.

**Avoid:** Full architecture diagrams. If the audience needs more, they'll ask.

**Time:** 2 minutes.

## Slide 6: Why Now / Why Crypto

**What goes on it:** What changed that makes this possible (or necessary) right now.

**Structure:**
- One technology shift (e.g., "EIP-4844 reduced L2 costs 10x")
- One market shift (e.g., "40K new wallets on Base in Q1")
- One regulatory or cultural shift (if relevant)

**For non-crypto audiences:** Add a brief explanation of why this needs to be onchain.

**Avoid:** "Blockchain is the future" — say what's different about today vs. 2021.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 7: Traction

**What goes on it:** Real numbers, dated, sourced.

**Structure:**
- 3-4 metrics that show growth
- A chart if the trend is clear
- Links to on-chain data if applicable

**Good metrics:** Active users, TVL, fees collected, transaction count, retention rate.

**Bad metrics:** Discord members, Twitter followers, "wallets created" (without activity).

**If you have weak traction:** Lead with insight or team credentials instead.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 8: Market

**What goes on it:** Specific, defensible market size with a path.

**Structure:**
- Count the actual addressable users (not "everyone with a wallet")
- Show the revenue path (TAM × your take rate)
- Cite your source

**Avoid:** "TAM: $1T DeFi market." That's not a market size — that's a number someone made up.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 9: Business Model

**What goes on it:** How money flows.

**Structure:**
- Revenue source (fees, subscription, token, etc.)
- Take rate (percentage of value captured)
- Unit economics (if you have them)
- Projected revenue at key milestones

**For protocols:** Show fee flow from user → protocol → token holders (if applicable).

**Avoid:** Vague models ("we'll figure out monetization later").

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 10: Competition

**What goes on it:** Who else is doing this and how you differ.

**Structure:**
- 2-4 competitors, named
- One key differentiator per competitor
- What you do that none of them do

**Format:** A comparison table or a 2-axis positioning chart.

**Avoid:** "We have no competitors." If the problem is real, someone is trying to solve it.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 11: Team

**What goes on it:** Why this team wins.

**Structure per person:**
- Name / pseudonym
- Role
- One most-relevant prior credential
- One area of expertise

**For pseudonymous teams:** Show GitHub, prior shipped contracts, on-chain track record.

**Avoid:** Listing every job a team member has had. Pick the one that's relevant to this project.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Slide 12: Ask

**What goes on it:** What you want and what you'll do with it.

**Structure:**
- Amount and round type ("$1.5M Seed")
- Use of funds (percentages across categories)
- 18-month milestones funded by this round
- Who's leading (if known)

**Avoid:** Vague asks ("we're raising a round"). Be specific.

**Time:** 1 minute.

## Appendix Slides (keep in reserve)

- Technical deep-dive (architecture, contract design)
- Tokenomics (if relevant to the audience)
- Financial model (for VCs)
- Roadmap timeline (for community)
- Risk analysis (for technical audiences)
- Competitive analysis detail
- Team resumes (if asked)
