# Audience Types

Different deck audiences require different structures, emphasis, and depth. Building the wrong deck for the audience is the most common deck failure.

## VCs / Investors

**Goal:** Get a 30-minute meeting or a term sheet.

**What they care about:**
- Market size and your defensible position in it
- Traction (users, revenue, TVL, fees)
- Team's ability to execute
- Why now (what changed that makes this possible)

**Structure:** Standard 10-12 slide deck (see `slide-structure.md`).

**Depth:** Surface-level on tech, deep on market and business model. VCs don't need to understand your zk-rollup architecture — they need to know the TAM and your moat.

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Opening with tokenomics (save for appendix or Q&A)
- More than 15 slides (attention runs out)
- No clear ask or milestone (they don't know what you want)

## Hackathon Judges

**Goal:** Survive the 3-minute filter.

**What they care about:**
- Does it work? (show it)
- Is it interesting?
- Does it use sponsor tech?

**Structure:** 6-8 slides max. Lead with the demo.

**Depth:** Technical depth wins points, but only if the demo works first. Show code snippets, contract addresses, and sponsor integration.

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- A deck without a product demo (judges skip)
- Explaining the problem for 3 minutes (show, don't tell)
- Selecting a sponsor prize without using their tech

## Partners / Integrators

**Goal:** Get them to integrate with you.

**What they care about:**
- What they get (API quality, uptime, user value)
- Integration effort (how hard is it to connect)
- Mutual benefit (what's in it for them)

**Structure:** 5-7 slides. API-first.

**Depth:** Deep on integration, light on vision. Include an API reference, sample calls, and a clear integration path.

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Pitching for funding instead of partnership
- Vague API promises without actual endpoints
- Making the integration sound complex

## Community / Token Holders

**Goal:** Build trust and excitement.

**What they care about:**
- Vision and roadmap
- Token utility and value accrual
- Team credibility
- Transparency

**Structure:** 8-12 slides. More narrative-driven.

**Depth:** Balance between vision and specifics. Community decks need to be compelling to both technical and non-technical audiences.

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Promising dates you can't hit (community remembers)
- Hiding risks or challenges (transparency builds trust)
- Over-complicating tokenomics (confusion = distrust)

## Internal / Team Alignment

**Goal:** Get the team on the same page.

**What they care about:**
- Priorities and timeline
- Resource allocation
- Success metrics

**Structure:** 5-8 slides. Actionable.

**Depth:** Deep on specifics, light on narrative. This is a planning tool, not a pitch.

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Vague goals ("increase users" vs. "10K users by Q3")
- No clear ownership (who does what)
- Missing success metrics (how do we know we won)

## Adapting the Canonical Deck

The 10-12 slide canonical structure (see `slide-structure.md`) is your starting point. Adapt:

| Audience | Add | Remove | Expand |
|---|---|---|---|
| VCs | Financial model, competition | Technical deep-dive | Market, traction |
| Hackathon | Demo, tech stack | Market analysis | Product, sponsor tech |
| Partners | API reference, integration path | Team slide | Integration details |
| Community | Roadmap, token utility | Competition | Vision, transparency |
| Internal | Timeline, ownership | Narrative | Metrics, priorities |
